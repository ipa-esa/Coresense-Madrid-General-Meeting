Meeting Venue

The CoreSense General Meeting takes place at the Universidad Rey Juan Carlos (URJC), Campus de Aranjuez. Aranjuez is a UNESCO World Heritage Royal City located 47 km south of Madrid, in a natural setting at the confluence of the Tagus and Jarama rivers.

How to Get There

🚉 By Train (recommended)

Aranjuez is well-served by Madrid's Cercanías (suburban rail) network. The C-3 line runs directly from central Madrid to Aranjuez station.

From Madrid Atocha

Line: C-3 (direction Aranjuez)

Journey time: ~42–48 min

Frequency: Every 15–30 min

Renfe C-3 timetable →

From Madrid Chamartín

Line: C-3 (direction Aranjuez, via Atocha)

Journey time: ~60–70 min

Frequency: Every 15–30 min

From Aranjuez station to campus

Taxi: 5–8 min drive, ~€5–7

On foot: ~20 min (1.7 km) heading north-west

Bus: Local line L2 (check local timetable)

Tip: Purchase a T-Casual (10-trip card, Zone B2) or a multi-trip card at any Cercanías station. Single tickets (Zone B2) can also be bought at ticket machines or the app Cercanías Madrid.

🚗 By Car

Aranjuez is easily reached from Madrid via the A-4 motorway (E-5). Take exit 37 – Aranjuez and follow signs to the city centre. The campus is on the northern side of the city.

Parking: Free parking is available on campus. GPS coordinates: 40.0429°N, 3.6032°W.

✈️ By Air

The nearest airport is Madrid Barajas (MAD). From the airport, take the Metro to Nuevos Ministerios or Atocha, then connect to the C-3 Cercanías line to Aranjuez. Total journey time from airport: ~90 min.

Alternatively, take a taxi or rideshare from the airport to Aranjuez (approximately 45 min, ~€45–55).

Social Activity – Palacio Real de Aranjuez

On the afternoon of Day 2, we will enjoy a guided visit to the Palacio Real de Aranjuez and its famous gardens, one of the most impressive Royal Palaces in Spain and part of the Aranjuez Cultural Landscape UNESCO World Heritage Site (inscribed 2001).

The palace, built in the 18th century, served as the spring residence of the Spanish monarchs and contains state rooms, the Porcelain Room, and a stunning collection of art and furniture. The surrounding Jardín del Príncipe and Jardín de la Isla extend along the banks of the Tagus river.

Practical Tips

Currency & Payments

Spain uses the Euro (€). Credit cards are widely accepted. ATMs are available in the city centre.

Climate

Aranjuez enjoys a semi-arid continental climate. Expect mild to warm temperatures. Check the forecast before travelling: the Tagus valley can be windy.

Language

Spanish (Castilian) is the local language. English is not widely spoken outside the university campus. A few basic phrases in Spanish will be appreciated.

Electricity

Type F sockets (Schuko), 230 V / 50 Hz. European plugs fit; visitors from the UK, US, etc. will need an adapter.

Emergency numbers

112 – European emergency number (police, fire, ambulance)

061 – Medical emergency (Spain)

Dining near campus

Several restaurants and cafeterias are available in Aranjuez city centre (~10–15 min from campus). The URJC campus cafeteria also provides light meals and coffee.
